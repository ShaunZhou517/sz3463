Chinese Calligraphy

The typical concept of East Asian calligraphy is "dipping ink, holding a brush, and writing Chinese characters." It emphasizes the calligraphy revealed by the lines. Calligraphy is considered an important art and the most elegant form of writing in East Asia. Writing good Chinese characters has always been a quality. It originated from Chinese calligraphy, respected by the civilizations of East Asia and widely practiced, including: China, Japan, Vietnam, and North Korea. Chinese calligraphy is the most typical representative of East Asian calligraphy, and it is also the general term for East Asian calligraphy. Because most of the written fonts are Chinese characters, all East Asian calligraphy is also the first to evolve from China. In addition to the four treasures of the traditional study, recent innovations in writing tools have led to the rise of hard pen calligraphy.
